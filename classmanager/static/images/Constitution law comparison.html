Module 1: Meaning of Constitution, Law & Constitutionalism

1. Understanding the Concepts

Constitution:

  • A constitution is the supreme law of a country.

  • It lays down the framework of government, its powers, duties, and the rights of citizens.

  • It is like a “rulebook” that governs how a nation functions.

Law:

  • Law is a set of rules made by the government to regulate behavior.

  • Laws apply to everyone equally and are enforceable through courts.

  • They are created within the framework of the constitution.

Constitutionalism:

  • Constitutionalism is the principle that the government must act according to the constitution.

  • It ensures the protection of citizens’ rights and limits arbitrary use of power.

  • It is more about “practice” than written rules—it reflects respect for law and democracy.
